I think we're a perfect match! Let me tell you why ...

I came across PostHog when your newest website design got posted on Hacker News in September and got a little tingly feeling. This is the kind of love and care and weirdness that I want to put into my work!

Your homepage makes me so happy, I can hardly put it into words. There is precious little delight and personality and joy in technology these days that when I see it, it jumps out at me. It's a labor of love in a world of optimization. All the little details - the commit history, the window management, the right-click - come together to show real care.

The delightful design encouraged me to keep digging, and I'm so thankful that I did because I found the thing that made me realize that we were meant for each other: The Handbook. Not only do our values align, the fact that it's all spelled out in detail means a great deal to me.

I wrote down my own (engineering-focused) values last year, but here are few overlaps that stood out to me.

  • I work hard to build a welcoming environment that lifts people up to every company I've worked at. Not only is it the right thing to do, it creates psychological safety that makes it easier to take chances, risk mistakes, and ship. A culture without that safety is fundamentally broken, in my experience.
  • Focusing on asynchronous communication and maker schedules is so hard to do without the whole company being aligned on that value. You have to write everything down, be thoughtful about how you're communicating, and put the effort in up front. I'm such a big fan of mindful communication that I pitched a book about it to a publishing company!

Notes help me clarify my thoughts (Paul Graham said it best), and publishing shows commitment to what I'm saying. Teams that put value into writing are more focused, care more about what they're actually producing, and push you to do better (rather than just more).

Since my time at Auth0, I've been looking for a developer-focused, people-first, weird company to pour myself into. The posted job descriptions have character and truly spoke to me. Genuine builders Grown ups Optimistic problem solvers Enthusiastic drivers. It all clicked into place as I found myself whispering "yes!" to myself as I read more and more.

I'm a genuine builder. I built a nano-scale software product company that made $35K/year and sold for ~2X. I built an open source, alpha-stage personal data pipeline and a CLI budgeting tool that powered our family for a few years. Most recently, I built a secure MCP gateway and an AI visibility dashboard that's launching with design partners this year. There are a number of other smaller projects on GitHub.

I'm a grown up. I really appreciate this phrasing and don't have anything add except you know it when you see it and I was smiling the whole time reading your welcoming environment list.

I'm an optimistic problem solver. I think a good indicator here is whether or not someone has burnt out before. I have (thanks COVID) so I know the feeling when things start to get overwhelming and what to do when I feel it. Making work visible, clear communication in all directions, and breaking things down goes a long way towards restoring sanity.

I'm an enthusiastic driver. I've lead projects of all shapes and sizes, from solo projects (see above) to team-wide products (ask me about SDK telemetry, the Auth0 Marketplace, or guided customer onboarding) to company-wide initiatives (ask me about the Okta VPN deployment or the Platform One deployment).

Everything screamed at me "APPLY HERE NOW!" As I was getting my ducks (well, notes) in a row, I saw the final thing that pushed me over the edge. You're looking for IAM experience! That's my bread and butter.

Where do I even start?! I found my love for identity at Auth0, where I started out building SDKs for our consumer identity platform. I taught identity to engineering and tech-adjacent new employees, mentored by the late and truly great Vittorio Bertucci. I wrote an OpenID Connect authorization server liveProject for Manning Publications. I built the federated authentication system for the Auth0 partner platform. I built and helped partners build SAML integrations for the Auth0 Marketplace. I built identity and authorization systems for Department of Defense staff. Most recently, I wrote a proof-of-concept that used employee identities for AI agents calling external APIs.

Since Thursday, I've spent a bunch time immersing myself in everything PostHog, and I just keeping finding reasons that we should be together.

Keys and stickers showed up Monday!

I truly appreciate your interview process. I've interviewed many people in the last several years, and a long, drawn-out, overly rigorous process takes up so much time on both ends, and doesn't seem to produce much better results. Hiring is hard, but I appreciate how deliberate and succinct that your process is.

Here's what else I bring to the table:

  • I have great discernment about getting to value as fast as possible, whether that's learning, revenue, or customer enjoyment.
  • I have a long history in developer experience, from open source libraries and SDKs to partner integration tools and pro-code. I love working with and for developers, figuring out the best way to get them to fall into the "pit of success."
  • I love that your marketing is all content. I love to write (obviously) and I would be so proud of have my words on your website. Inbound FTW.

Thank you for reading through this and giving me a chance!
