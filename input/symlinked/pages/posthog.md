I think we're a perfect match! Let me tell you why ... 

I came across **PostHog** when your newest website design got posted on Hacker News in September and got a little tingly feeling. This is the kind of [love and care and weirdness](#love) that I want to put into my work!

> Your homepage makes me so happy, I can hardly put it into words. There is precious little delight and personality and joy in technology there days that when I see it, it jumps out at me. It's a labor of love in a world of optimization. All the little details - the commit history, the window management, the right-click - come together to show real care. 

The delightful design encouraged me to keep digging and I'm so thankful that I did because I found the thing that made me realize that we were meant for each other: **The Handbook**. Not only do our [values align](#values) but just the fact that it's all spelled out in detail [means a great deal](#meaning) to me.

> I wrote down my own (engineering-focused) values [here](/values/) but a few overlaps that stood out to me ...
> 
> A welcoming environment where people lift each other up is what I work hard to build at every place I work. That creates psychological safety that makes it easier to take chances, risk mistakes, and ship things. 
> 
> Focusing on asynchronous communication and maker schedules is so hard to do without the whole company being aligned on that value. You have to write things down, be thoughtful about how you're communicating, and put the effort in up front. I'm such a big fan of asynchronous communication that I pitched [a book about it](/asynchronous-communication/) to a publishing company! 

> I write a lot, both because [notes](/notes/) help me clarify what I'm thinking ([Paul Graham said it best](https://paulgraham.com/words.html)) and [publishing](/posts/) shows public commitment to what I'm saying. I have worked at places that put value into writing things down and places that don't. The former are more focused, care more about what they're actually producing, and push you to do better (rather than just [more](https://www.bennorthrop.com/Essays/2021/always-do-extra.php)). **Write it down** is one of my own important values and something I push everyone around me to do more.

[Since my time at Auth0](https://www.joshcanhelp.com/goodbye-auth0/), I've been looking for a developer-focused, people-first, weird company to pour myself into. I started reading through relevant job listings recently and loved what I saw. [Genuine builders](#build) ... [Grown ups](#adult) ... [Optimistic problem solvers](#solve) ... [Enthusiastic drivers](#drive). It all clicked into place as I found myself whispering "yes!" to myself as I read more and more.

> **I'm a genuine builder.** I've built lots of things on my own: a nano-scale software product company ($35K/year, sold for $60K); a[personal data pipeline](/personal-data-pipeline/) (open source, alpha stage); a secure MCP gateway (proof of concept, not quite alpha); an AI visibility dashboard (beta launch this year); a [CLI budgeting tool](/budget-cli/) (open source, powered our family for a couple of years), and lots more smaller things on [GitHub](https://github.com/joshcanhelp?tab=repositories)

> **I'm a grown up.** I really appreciate this phrasing and don't have much to add except you know it when you see it and I smiling the whole time reading your [welcoming environment list](https://posthog.com/handbook/company/grown-ups).

> **I'm an optimistic problem solver.** I think a good indicator here is whether or not someone has burnt out before. I have (thanks COVID) so I know that feeling when things start to get overwhelming and I know what to do when that starts to happen. Making work visible, clear communication in all directions, and breaking things down goes a long way towards restoring sanity.

> **I'm an enthusiastic driver.** I've lead projects of all shapes and sizes, from solo projects (see above) to team-wide products (ask me about SDK telemetry, the Auth0 Marketplace, or guided customer onboarding) to company-wide initiatives (ask me about the Okta VPN deployment or the [Platform One](https://p1.dso.mil) deployment). 

And that's not even including the whole [pineapple on pizza](#pineapple) thing!

Everything screamed at me "APPLY HERE NOW!" As I was getting my ducks (well, notes) in a row, I saw the final thing that pushed me over the edge. **You're looking for identity experience!** That's my [bread and butter](#butter).

> *Where do I even start?!* I found my love for identity at Auth0, where I started out building SDKs for our consumer identity platform. I taught identity to engineering and tech adjacent new employees, mentored by the late and truly great [Vittorio Bertucci](https://www.joshcanhelp.com/vittorio/). I wrote an OpenID Connect authorization server liveProject for [Manning Publications](https://www.manning.com/liveprojectseries/federation-and-sign-on-ser). I built the federated authentication system for our partner platform. I built and helped build several of the SAML SSO integrations in the [Auth0 Marketplace](https://marketplace.auth0.com/features/sso-integrations). I built identity and authorization systems for DoD employees and a PoC of agentic identity for the startup that I'm currently at.

Since Thursday, I've spent a bunch time immersing myself in [everything PostHog](#merch) and I just keeping finding [reasons](#reasons) that we should be together.  

> Stickers and keycaps are on the way! Order #7368. 

> I truly appreciate your interview process. I've [interviewed many people](https://www.joshcanhelp.com/technical-interview/) in the last several years and a long, drawn-out, overly rigorous process takes up so much time on both end and doesn't seem to produce much better results. Hiring is hard but I appreciate how deliberate and succinct that your process is.

Here's what else I bring to the table:

- I have great discernment about getting to value as fast as possible, whether that's learning, revenue, or customer enjoyment. 
- I have a long history in developer experience, from open source libraries and SDKs to partner integration tools and pro-code . I love working with and for developers, figuring out the best way to get them to fall into the "pit of success."
- I love that your marketing is all content and I love to write. I would be so proud of have my words on your website. Inbound FTW!

Thank you for reading through this and giving me a chance! I think **PostHog** would be a great place for me professionally and I'm sure I'd be a natural fit for your team.
